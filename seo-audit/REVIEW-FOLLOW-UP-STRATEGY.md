# Google Review Follow-Up Strategy — Knight Group

**Purpose:** Grow review volume without annoying customers, violating Google policies, or creating awkward family-business dynamics.

**Not in scope yet:** Automated SMS blasts, third-party “review gating” tools, or incentive offers (Google prohibits reviews in exchange for discounts).

---

## What you already have working

- Strong on-site proof (gallery, FAQs, GBP link on homepage/about)
- Owner-led jobs — Vince can ask in person when the job went well
- You already nudge after jobs and follow up with some customers manually

The gap is **consistency and timing**, not motivation.

---

## Principles (keep it simple)

1. **Ask only after a clearly successful job** — customer satisfied, mess cleaned up, walkthrough done.
2. **One polite ask, one reminder max** — never chase people who ignored the first message.
3. **Make it easy** — direct link to your Google review form (same `cid=` URL already on the site).
4. **No scripts that sound corporate** — Vince’s voice, short, grateful.
5. **Never offer payment or discounts for reviews** — Google TOS violation and looks bad locally.
6. **Separate “feedback” from “public review”** — if something went wrong, fix it privately first; do not send unhappy customers to Google.

---

## Recommended flow (manual, low-tech)

### At job close (best conversion)

Vince (or whoever closed the job) says:

> “If the work looks good to you, a Google review really helps a small local business like ours. I can text you the link — no pressure at all.”

If yes → send the link **same day** while memory is fresh.

### Same-day text (from Vince or business line)

```
Hi [Name], Vince from Knight Group — thanks again for having us out today.
If you have a minute, a Google review helps other Pinellas homeowners find us:
https://www.google.com/maps?cid=10508624668428370015
Either way, call us anytime you need something else.
```

### One reminder (optional, 5–7 days later)

Only if they agreed at close but didn’t review:

```
Hi [Name], quick follow-up from Knight Group — no rush.
If you’re willing to share a sentence on Google, here’s the link again: [URL]
Thanks again — Vince
```

Stop there.

---

## Who should ask

| Situation | Who asks |
|-----------|----------|
| Vince on site, happy customer | Vince at close + text same day |
| You (office) know job went well from Vince’s update | You text with Vince’s name |
| Customer already emailed praise | Reply thanking them + review link |
| Job had issues / warranty touch-up pending | **Do not ask** until resolved |

Avoid **you** texting people Vince just met if the relationship is purely Vince’s — it can feel like a call center. Better: Vince sends, or you send “Vince asked me to pass this along…”

---

## Tracking (spreadsheet — no software required)

Columns: Date | Customer | Job type | City | Asked? (Y/N) | Review posted? (check monthly) | Notes

Review monthly in GBP — mark who converted. Patterns emerge (e.g., plumbing jobs vs small punch lists).

---

## What NOT to build yet

- **Automated review SMS platforms** — often feel spammy; hard to pause for problem jobs
- **Email sequences to every lead** — includes estimates that never booked
- **QR codes on invoices only** — fine as supplement, but in-person ask + text wins for handyman
- **“Review us on Yelp/Facebook/Google” walls** — pick **Google** first for local SEO; don’t split focus

Revisit automation only if manual flow runs 3+ months and volume still stalls.

---

## Policy-safe language on the website

Keep saying **registered and insured** — not licensed (unless referring to trades you refer out to).

Review asks should never imply you’re licensed as a plumber/GC.

---

## Success targets (realistic)

| Timeframe | Goal |
|-----------|------|
| 30 days | 2–3 new Google reviews from manual flow |
| 90 days | 10+ total reviews (from ~7 today) |
| 12 months | 25+ reviews with steady monthly additions |

Quality and recency matter more than total count for local pack visibility.
