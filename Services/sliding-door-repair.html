Patio and closet slider components we service

Sliding systems fail gradually — first a little drag, then a jump off track, then a latch that no longer meets the strike.

  • Nylon and steel roller assemblies sized to panel weight
  • Track cleaning, straightening, and localized replacement sections
  • Height adjustment screws at head and sill
  • Lock and handle kits on foot-traffic doors
  • Bug seal and brush sweeps at sill gaps
  • Parallel alignment of fixed and active panels
  • Screen insert repairs sharing the same track
  • Security pin or charley bar alignment on secondary doors

  • Doors that jump off track or bind at the header

Repair before full replacement

Many homeowners assume a sticking slider means a new door system. Often roller kits, track cleaning, and height adjustments restore smooth travel for years. When glass is compromised or frames are structurally failed, we explain replacement options honestly.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Common questions homeowners ask before booking this type of work in Pinellas County.

Why does my patio slider stick even after cleaning the track?

Worn rollers, bent tracks, or settled framing often cause binding beyond surface dirt. We inspect rollers, height adjustment, and track condition on site.

Can you replace sliding door rollers?

Yes on many standard patio and closet doors when compatible roller kits are available for your panel weight and track type.

Do you repair closet bypass doors?

Yes — bypass closets with jumped rollers or damaged tracks are common small scopes.

Should I replace the whole door if the glass is fine?

Not necessarily. Roller, track, and latch repairs restore function at lower cost when frames and glass are sound.

Do you fix sliding screen panels too?

Yes — screen panels often share tracks with glass doors and are repaired alongside adjustment work.

Which cities do you serve for sliding door repair?

Pinellas County — including Safety Harbor, Clearwater, Largo, and Seminole.
