Sink and faucet symptoms we diagnose first

Not every drip needs a new faucet. We trace moisture paths — supply vs. drain vs. condensation — before recommending parts.

  • Continuous drip at spout or base indicating cartridge or seat wear
  • Water under the trap from loose slip nuts or cracked tailpieces
  • Discolored cabinet floors from slow supply seepage
  • Reduced flow when aerators or supply valves partially close
  • Loose deck plates on stone tops that channel water sideways
  • Disposal vibration loosening drain connections on double bowls
  • Old polybutylene or corroded copper transitions at stops
  • Soap dispenser holes leaking when unused ports are unsealed
