Joint types we strip and renew

  • Lavatory backsplash and countertop seams
  • Window stool and exterior brickmold perimeters
  • Door threshold and sidelite transitions

  • Ledger boards on lanais and porch ceilings

  • Sink deck penetrations on stone and composite tops

Product selection for wet zones

Mildew-resistant silicones and urethanes rated for immersion outperform painter's caulk in showers. We tool beads concave where water should shed and allow cure times before first use.

Caulking repair for tubs, showers, windows, and exteriors

Failed caulk lines let humidity into drywall and framing long before you see stains. Knight Group removes failing sealant, cleans substrates, and applies mildew-resistant products suited to Florida caulking repair handyman scopes at tubs, sinks, windows, and doors.

  • Tub and shower corners where separation invites mold

Prep beats product marketing

New caulk over old mildew fails quickly. We dry cavities, use appropriate backer rod where gaps are wide, and tool beads for water-shedding profiles. Homeowners planning tile or glass surrounds should caulk after those trades settle.

Caulk failure patterns in humid Pinellas baths

Guest baths in Clearwater rentals often fail at tub corners first — grout looks fine while silicone behind tile lets moisture into drywall. We remove failing material fully before new beads.

Exterior caulk at stucco-to-trim joints should be scheduled before summer rains saturate penetrations — especially on west-facing walls.

Frequently asked questions

Common questions homeowners ask before booking this type of work in Pinellas County.

How often should tub caulk be replaced in Florida?

Many baths need refresh every few years due to humidity and flexing — sooner if mold or separation appears.

Do you remove old caulk before applying new?

Yes — failing caulk and loose material are removed for adhesion.

Can you caulk exterior windows and doors?

Yes — exterior sealant against driven rain is a common handyman scope.

Which caulk do you use in showers?

Mildew-resistant silicone or specified bath products suited to wet areas.

Can caulking prevent water damage repairs later?

Proper seals reduce intrusion — but active leaks from plumbing still need fixture repair first.

Do you caulk kitchen backsplashes?

Yes — counter-to-wall and sink ledge joints are included in many kitchen refresh scopes.
