Drain locations we clear within handyman scope

Accessibility and branch-line depth determine whether a handyman auger suffices or a plumbing contractor with main-line equipment is required.

  • Kitchen double-bowl traps with grease accumulation
  • Lavatory pop-up assemblies tangled with hair
  • Tub shoe connections behind removable overflow plates
  • Laundry tubs catching lint and fabric softener residue
  • Utility sinks in garages and craft rooms
  • Disposal drain lines after jam events
  • Outdoor kitchen sinks with limited cleanout access
  • Bar sinks on island loops with long horizontal runs (assessed on site)

When snaking is not enough

Recurring clogs in the same branch, gurgling in multiple fixtures, or sewage odor at cleanouts signal main-line issues. We stop before damaging old pipes and refer licensed plumbers with camera gear when appropriate.

Drain unclogging within handyman scope

Drain unclogging handyman work covers slow kitchen and bath drains when the blockage sits in accessible traps and branch lines — not when the main sewer requires a contractor with jetter equipment. Knight Group clears many P-traps, pop-up assemblies, and laundry standpipes across Pinellas County.

Drains we commonly open

  • Kitchen sinks with grease and disposal debris in traps
  • Lavatory pop-ups tangled with hair below the stopper
  • Tub/shower combos with accessible drum traps or removable grates
  • Laundry and utility tubs with sock and lint clogs near the trap
  • Garage floor drains when grates lift and lines are reachable

When we refer a licensed plumber

Multiple fixtures backing up, sewage odors at the cleanout, or recurring clogs after camera evidence usually mean main-line work. We explain that clearly and can coordinate drywall or access panel restoration after licensed clearing.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Common questions homeowners ask before booking this type of work in Pinellas County.

Can a handyman unclog a sink drain?

Yes when blockages are in traps or accessible branch lines — not when main sewers back up throughout the house.

Do you clear slow bathtub drains?

Often yes when stoppers and traps are reachable. Persistent main line clogs need a plumbing contractor.

Will you use a snake on kitchen drains?

Hand augers and trap disassembly are used when appropriate for accessible clogs.

What if multiple fixtures back up at once?

That pattern suggests main line issues — we refer licensed plumbers instead of masking symptoms.

Can you fix a garbage disposal clog too?

Simple jams and trap clogs related to disposals are common combined scopes.

Do you repair pipes damaged during clearing?

Minor trap component replacements may be included; extensive pipe replacement is quoted separately.
