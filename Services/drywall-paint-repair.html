Unified finish steps from patch to final coat

Visible repair squares usually mean texture or sheen mismatch — not bad mud work alone.

  • Feathered joint compound over screws and seams
  • Sanding between coats with dust control in occupied rooms
  • Knockdown or orange peel replication on ceilings
  • Stain-blocking primer on nicotine, tannin, and water marks
  • Cut-in brush work at ceilings and trim before rolling walls
  • Sheen matched to adjacent surfaces — flat vs. eggshell vs. satin
  • Touch-up labeling for future maintenance in rental units
  • Cleanup of masking, drop cloths, and fixture protection

Color matching expectations

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Common questions homeowners ask before booking this type of work in Pinellas County.

Can you patch drywall and paint the same area?

Yes — that is the focus of this service. We mud, texture if needed, prime, and topcoat for a unified finish.

Will the paint match my existing color?

We blend with on-site testing when original cans are unavailable. Sheen and age affect matches — we discuss expectations before starting.

Do you move furniture for drywall paint repairs?

We ask you to clear small areas when possible and protect remaining items with drop cloths. Larger moves can be noted when booking.

Is texture included in drywall and paint repair?

Light texture blending is included when needed. Heavy ceiling resprays may be quoted separately.

How do you handle water stains before painting?

Stain-blocking primer is applied after source moisture is resolved so tannins do not bleed through topcoats.

Can landlords book multiple unit touch-ups?

Yes — provide unit addresses and photo sets for batch scheduling where routes allow.
