# Knight Group Handyman Services — Phase 1 On-Call Tech Strategy

## Core strategy

Knight Group should create an **On-Call Approved Tech Network** before hiring W-2 employees.

The business model:

> Knight Group generates the lead, controls the CRM, schedules the customer, collects payment, documents the job, and pays an approved on-call tech after completion.

This solves the current problem: if the main field worker is unavailable, Knight Group can still accept small handyman jobs and keep a profit margin.

---

## Current recommended structure

### Use this first

**1099 / independent on-call subcontractor bench**

Best for:
- occasional overflow jobs
- small handyman work
- jobs the main field worker cannot take
- testing whether the CRM is producing enough volume
- generating Knight Group revenue without payroll overhead

### Do not start with this yet

**W-2 employees**

Use W-2 later only after job volume is consistent enough to justify payroll, workers’ comp, taxes, onboarding, wage tracking, and employee management.

---

## Job scope guardrails

Knight Group should focus on **minor handyman, repair, and maintenance work**.

Good Phase 1 jobs:
- drywall patches
- minor painting/touch-ups
- furniture assembly
- shelf/mirror/TV mounting where allowed
- caulking
- pressure washing
- minor fixture replacement
- punch-list repairs
- basic property maintenance
- non-structural, non-permit work

Avoid unless handled by a licensed contractor:
- electrical
- plumbing
- HVAC
- roofing
- structural framing
- major remodeling
- screen enclosure structural/aluminum work
- permit-required work
- anything outside the handyman/minor maintenance lane

Rule:

> If it likely requires a license or permit, Knight Group should not act as the prime contractor unless the correct licensed contractor is responsible.

## Customer payment flow

Preferred flow:

1. Lead comes into CRM.
2. Customer is screened by phone/text.
3. Knight Group sends written rate authorization by text.
4. Customer replies “Approved.”
5. Knight Group assigns on-call tech.
6. Tech completes job.
7. Tech sends photos, time, materials, and notes.
8. Knight Group sends invoice/payment link.
9. Customer pays Knight Group.
10. Knight Group pays tech after payment clears.

Important:

> Customer payment should go to Knight Group, not directly to the tech.

This keeps:
- customer relationship
- revenue history
- proof of collections
- bookkeeping
- tech payout tracking
- margin control

## Existing hourly pricing model

For small jobs, use written **rate authorization**, not a detailed estimate.

Standard service call:

> $150 first hour, then $75 per additional hour, plus approved materials and material handling/markup.

Specialty/higher-liability service call:

> $200 first hour, then $100 per additional hour, plus approved materials and material handling/markup.

Emergency:

> Emergency fee added on top of hourly service rate.

## Tech payment model

### Preferred model: fixed job payout

Best because it avoids arguments.

Example:

| Customer charge | Materials | Tech payout | Knight Group gross margin |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| $300 | $25 | $190 | $85 |
| $450 | $50 | $300 | $100 |
| $625 | $75 | $350 | $200 |

Use this when you can estimate the tech’s effort before assigning the job.

### Backup model: labor percentage split

Use this for simple hourly jobs.

Recommended split:

> Tech gets 65–75% of collected labor. Knight Group keeps 25–35%.

Example:

| Customer labor charge | Tech at 70% | Knight Group at 30% |
|---:|---:|---:|
| $150 | $105 | $45 |
| $225 | $157.50 | $67.50 |
| $300 | $210 | $90 |
| $450 | $315 | $135 |

Do not promise 80% unless the tech is excellent, fully independent, and brings their own tools/insurance/reliability.

### Materials rule

Best options:
1. Customer pays materials directly.
2. Knight Group buys materials and marks them up.
3. Tech buys materials only with pre-approval and receipt.

Do not allow unapproved material spending.

---

## Cash payment policy

Cash is allowed only if controlled.

Rule:

> Cash is paid to Knight Group, not personally to the tech.

Cash process:
1. Customer receives invoice/receipt.
2. Tech reports cash collected before leaving.
3. Tech sends photo of cash + job address + amount.
4. Customer receives receipt by text/email.
5. Tech turns in cash before payout.
6. Knight Group deposits cash into business account.
7. Tech payout is calculated after cash is logged.

Best practical rule for new techs:

> No cash on subcontractor jobs unless the tech is trusted.

## Tech onboarding requirements

Before assigning any job, collect:

- W-9
- signed subcontractor agreement
- photo ID
- phone/email/address
- service territory
- own tools confirmation
- own transportation confirmation
- service categories they can perform
- minimum job payout
- availability
- proof of general liability insurance if available
- workers’ comp coverage/exemption status if applicable
- emergency contact
- signed customer non-solicitation agreement
- signed no licensed/permit work agreement

Rule:

> No paperwork = no jobs.

---

## Subcontractor agreement terms to include

Must include:

- Tech is an independent contractor/subcontractor.
- Tech may accept or decline jobs.
- Tech provides own tools, transportation, phone, and work methods.
- Tech is responsible for taxes, insurance, licenses, and legal compliance.
- Tech may work for other companies/customers.
- Tech will not perform work requiring a license, permit, or trade certification unless properly licensed and approved in writing.
- Tech will not collect payment directly unless authorized.
- Tech must submit before/after photos, time, materials, and completion notes.
- Payment is owed only after customer payment clears.
- Tech may not solicit Knight Group customers directly for 12 months.
- Tech must behave professionally and protect Knight Group’s reputation.
- Tech is responsible for correcting poor workmanship if caused by their work.
- Knight Group may remove tech from the approved list at any time.

## Customer work authorization terms

For customer approval texts/forms/invoices:

- Pricing is hourly unless written fixed quote is provided.
- Final cost depends on actual time on site and approved materials.
- Payment is due at completion.
- No checks.
- Customer must approve additional work before it begins.
- Knight Group provides minor handyman/maintenance services only.
- Licensed or permit-required work may require referral to a properly licensed contractor.

Suggested language:

```text
Knight Group Handyman Services LLC provides minor handyman, repair, and maintenance services. Some work may be limited to non-structural, non-permit work. Licensed trade work may require referral to a properly licensed contractor.
```


## Pros of Phase 1 on-call tech bench

- You stop losing jobs when the main worker is unavailable.
- Knight Group starts profiting from the CRM/marketing system.
- Builds business revenue history.
- Gives vendor-list customers faster response.
- Avoids premature payroll.
- Easier to scale/test than W-2 hiring.
- Lets you learn real job economics.
- Creates leverage for your role as the business builder/manager.

---

## Cons / risks

- Less control than employees.
- Tech quality can damage the brand.
- Insurance exposure if setup is wrong.
- Customer may blame Knight Group for tech mistakes.
- Worker classification risk if managed like employees.
- Cash leakage risk.
- Tech may try to bypass Knight Group.
- Cannot safely expand into licensed/permit work without proper licensed partners.
- Requires job-level bookkeeping discipline.

## Launch sequence

### Step 1 — Internal setup

- Confirm payment system goes to Knight Group.
- Confirm business bank/payment accounts.
- Create tech agreement.
- Create customer rate authorization text.
- Create CRM job fields.
- Create cash policy.
- Create payout formula.
- Confirm insurance questions.

### Step 2 — Recruit

- Post Craigslist/Facebook/local ad.
- Screen 5–10 applicants.
- Pick 1–2 strong candidates.
- Collect W-9/paperwork.
- Confirm service area and job types.

### Step 3 — Test

- Assign one small job.
- Require photos/completion notes.
- Confirm payment flow works.
- Pay tech promptly.
- Grade tech.

### Step 4 — Scale carefully

- Add more job types only after process works.
- Keep licensed/permit work separate.
- Move to W-2 only after steady volume.

## Strategic summary

Knight Group’s best Phase 1 growth strategy is to become the **lead-generation, scheduling, payment, and customer-management layer** for small handyman jobs, while using vetted on-call techs for execution.

The company should not rush into W-2 employees yet. The first goal is to prove:

1. the CRM can generate jobs;
2. customers will pay Knight Group directly;
3. techs can complete jobs reliably;
4. Knight Group can keep a margin;
5. the process can run without the main field worker always being available.

Once that is proven, Knight Group can decide whether to hire a real part-time field employee.
